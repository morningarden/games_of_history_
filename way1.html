In the Middle of Learning

On this page, I present games that can be used as an introduction to learning history or as a summary along the way. I generally enjoy it as an introduction to a subject and then start learning.

◆◇GALLERY◇◆

    The birth of mankind

  • >>Click for larger image<<

    • ERA: prehistory
    • LEVEL: easy
    • TIME: 50min
    • This allows us to experience human evolution.
    • The game is designed to allow you to earn words as you earn cards. As you gain words, you can do more things and experience the evolution of mankind.
    • I used this game to open my world history class. Not only is it a fun way to learn about prehistory, but it is also a great ice-breaker. Prehistory is uncertain, so this is about as good as it gets.
    • ×

    CATAN-HISTORIES-

  • >>Click for larger image<<

    • ERA: 19th century
    • LEVEL: difficult
    • TIME: 90min
    • This allows us to experience people moving in old USA.
    • This game is a historical version of the famous 'Catan'. It offers a taste of the American frontier movement, but the rules are complex and time-consuming.
    • I ran it as a team game. During the game, the boy who made it to the end of the game said, "The only next step is Hawaii", which allowed me to expand the conversation to the American colonial movement.
    • ×

    The Risk

  • >>Click for larger image<<

    • ERA: 18-19th century
    • LEVEL: difficult
    • TIME: 100min
    • This allows us to experience competition for territory.
    • The game is played by moving the soldiers' frames to expand their territory. The game was well received by pupils who were familiar with military and national affairs, as they moved them on a real map.
    • I used this as an introduction to the formation of the nation-state. The original rules were difficult to follow, so I greatly simplified and played with conditions to match actual military power.
